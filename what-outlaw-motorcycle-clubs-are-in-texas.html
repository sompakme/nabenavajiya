What Outlaw Motorcycle Clubs Are In Texas

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, September 18, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

So You Wanna Know About Texas Biker Gangs? Buckle Up, Buttercup!

Texas. Home of wide-open spaces, ten-gallon hats, and apparently, a whole heap of folks who like their motorcycles loud, their rides chromed-out, and their brotherhood...well, let's just say it's a tight-knit bunch. Now, if you're curious about the motorcycle clubs that rumble down the highways here, then you might be knee-deep in curiosity about outlaw motorcycle clubs (OMCs).

Hold on, what's the difference between an OMC and your average weekend biker crew? Great question, my friend. Think of weekend warriors as folks who enjoy a scenic Sunday cruise and maybe a pit stop for a plate of ribs. Outlaw motorcycle clubs? Let's just say their definition of "scenic" might involve a less-than-touristy part of town, and their idea of a pit stop involves...well, let's just leave it to your imagination.

The Lone Star State's Big Shots: The Bandidos and the Cossacks

Texas boasts two of the most famous (or infamous, depending on your perspective) OMCs in the world: The Bandidos and The Cossacks. These guys are like the rockstars of the biker world, minus the spandex and with a whole lot more leather.

  • The Bandidos: Founded way back in 1966 (by a dockworker, no less!), these guys are a global force with chapters scattered all over the world. They're known for being fiercely loyal and, ahem, having a fondness for a certain kind of "business" that isn't exactly legal.
  • The Cossacks: These folks are a bit more tight-lipped about their history, but they've been around for decades and have a reputation for being just as tough as the Bandidos.

Now, before you start picturing epic brawls every time you see a Harley on the highway, let's be real. Most OMC members are just regular folks who enjoy the camaraderie of the biker lifestyle. That being said, there's always a chance you might stumble upon a "club run" where a whole pack of these leather-clad riders roll through town. Just a heads up: maybe dim your high beams and avoid any sudden lane changes.

Beyond the Big Names: Texas Has More Biker Flavor

While the Bandidos and Cossacks steal the spotlight, Texas has a whole scene of OMCs. There's a certain mystique to these clubs, but remember, it's important to be respectful. These guys take their brotherhood seriously, and showing respect goes a long way, even if you're not exactly interested in joining their ranks.

So there you have it, folks. A quick and dirty guide to Texas' outlaw motorcycle clubs. Remember, most of these guys are just out for a ride. But hey, a little knowledge never hurts, especially when it comes to understanding the unique culture of the Lone Star State. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have a sudden urge to crank up some ZZ Top and hit the open road...responsibly, of course.
