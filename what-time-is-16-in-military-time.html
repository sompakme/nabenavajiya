In military time, what time is it right now?

In military time, the hours are denoted by the numbers 00 through 2In this method, midnight is represented by the number 00, one a.m. by the number 01, one p.m. by the number 13, and so on. Minutes and seconds are expressed in precisely the same manner in both regular and military time. There is no difference in converting from regular to military time and vice versa; the minutes and seconds remain the same.

What time is it in military time at 12:30 a.m.?

Military time begins at 12 a.m. with the clock set to 0000 “zero hundred hours,” or “zero hundred minutes.” As a result, 12:30 a.m. would be 30 minutes after the clock began ticking at 0000, resulting in the time being 0030 on military time. 12:30 PM, on the other hand, is considered differently since it is twelve hours and thirty minutes into the day, which makes the military time 1230, and hence is treated differently.
