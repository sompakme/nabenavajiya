What Major Airports Are In Washington State

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, August 21, 2024

So You Wanna Fly to Washington? A Guide to the Evergreen State's Top Landing Pads

Washington state, oh Washington state! Land of misty forests, stunning mountains, and enough coffee to jumpstart a spaceship. But before you blast off with a venti latte in hand, you gotta figure out where you're gonna land, right? Don't worry, buddy, we've got you covered. This here is your one-stop shop to navigating the wild world of Washington's major airports.

The Big Leagues: Airports with International Flair

  • Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA): Buckle up, buttercup, because SEA is a whopper. We're talking the busiest airport in the Pacific Northwest, with connections to pretty much anywhere your wanderlust desires. Think flashing lights, bustling crowds, and enough shops to make your credit card cry (happy tears, hopefully).
  • Spokane International Airport (GEG): In the eastern heart of Washington lies GEG, a modern marvel that punches above its weight. Don't let the slightly less chaotic vibe fool you - this airport boasts connections to all the major hubs and is a great jumping-off point for exploring Spokane's outdoorsy delights.

The Underdogs: Serving Up Regional Charm

  • Bellingham International Airport (BLI): BLI might not be a heavyweight, but it packs a punch for affordability and convenience. Think smaller crowds, shorter security lines, and a gateway to the beautiful San Juan Islands. Plus, how cool is it to say you flew into an "international" airport? (Just don't tell anyone it's mostly to Vancouver, BC).
  • Tri-Cities Airport (PSC): Serving the Richland, Pasco, and Kennewick area, PSC is a great choice for those venturing into the heart of Washington's wine country. Plus, hey, who wouldn't want to fly into an airport named after "Tri-Cities"? Sounds futuristic!

Bonus Round: The "Don't Blink or You'll Miss It" Airports

Washington has a plethora of smaller airports catering to specific destinations. We're talking landing strips with stunning views and a local vibe that's thick enough to cut with a butter knife. But for the sake of brevity, let's just say if you're looking to land directly on a secluded beach or next to a charming mountain town, there's probably an airport out there with your name on it.

How to Choose Your Washington Airport:

  • Consider your destination: Are you hitting the big city lights of Seattle or the rolling hills of wine country? Different airports serve different vibes.
  • Think about budget: SEA might have the most connections, but it can also be the priciest. Consider smaller airports for a potentially lighter hit on the wallet.
  • Embrace the adventure! Washington has some truly unique landing spots. If you're feeling bold, explore the option of a smaller, more regional airport.

FAQ - Frequently Asked Flyer Woes

How to get to the airport from Seattle?

For SEA, there's a light rail that connects directly to the airport. For other airports, taxis, ride-sharing apps, or good old-fashioned rental cars are your best bet.

How to find parking at the airport?

Each airport has its own parking options. Most offer long-term, short-term, and even valet parking. Check the airport's website for details and booking options.

How early should I get to the airport?

Plan to arrive at least 2 hours before your domestic flight and 3 hours before an international flight. Better safe than missing your flight to paradise!

What can I bring on the plane?

The TSA (Transportation Security Administration) has the final say on what's allowed in your carry-on and checked baggage. Check their website for up-to-date guidelines before you pack.

How do I find out about flight delays or cancellations?

Airlines will typically notify you directly via email or text message. You can also check the airline's website or download their app for real-time updates.

So there you have it, folks! With this guide in your arsenal, navigating Washington's airports will be a breeze. Now get out there, explore the Evergreen State, and don't forget to pack your sense of adventure (and maybe a raincoat - it is Washington, after all).
