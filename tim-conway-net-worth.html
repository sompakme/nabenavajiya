What Is Tim Conway’s Net Worth?

Tim Conway was an American actor, comedian, writer, and director who had an estimated net worth of $15 million at the time of his death in 2019. However, it is believed that his net worth could have been higher had it not been for several legal battles and personal struggles. Despite this, his legacy as one of the greatest comedians of all time lives on.

Early Life and Education

Tim Conway was born on December 15, 1933, in Willoughby, Ohio. He grew up in Chagrin Falls and attended Chagrin Falls High School, where he was a standout athlete and student. After high school, he enlisted in the Army, where he served for two years.

After his service, Conway attended Bowling Green State University, where he majored in speech and radio. While in college, he discovered his passion for comedy and began performing at local nightclubs. He later moved to New York City to pursue a career in comedy.

Career

Tim Conway’s career began in the early 1960s, where he worked as a comedy writer for television shows such as “The Steve Allen Show” and “The David Frost Show.” He then moved on to work as a regular on “The Carol Burnett Show,” where he became known for his hilarious characters and impeccable timing.

Conway’s success on “The Carol Burnett Show” led to several television and film roles, including “McHale’s Navy,” “The Tim Conway Show,” and “Dorf on Golf.” He also lent his voice to various animated shows, such as “Scooby-Doo” and “SpongeBob SquarePants.”

Throughout his career, Conway won several awards for his comedic performances, including four Emmy Awards and a Golden Globe.

Personal Life

Tim Conway was married twice and had seven children. He was married to Mary Anne Dalton from 1961 to 1978, and together they had six children. He then married his second wife, Charlene Fusco, in 1984, and they had one child together.

Conway was also known for his close friendship with fellow comedian and “The Carol Burnett Show” co-star, Harvey Korman.

Real Estate

Tim Conway lived in Beverly Hills for over 30 years before moving to Encino, California. He owned several properties throughout his life, including a massive estate in Los Angeles, which he sold for $8.5 million in 2018.

Illness and Death

Tim Conway battled dementia for several years before his death on May 14, 2019, at the age of 85. His daughter, Kelly, released a statement shortly after his passing, saying, “The love he gave us, and the laughter he gave the world, will never be replaced but will be remembered forever.”

Legacy

Tim Conway’s legacy as one of the greatest comedians of all time lives on through his iconic performances on “The Carol Burnett Show” and various other film and television projects. He will always be remembered for his impeccable timing, hilarious characters, and unique brand of comedy.
