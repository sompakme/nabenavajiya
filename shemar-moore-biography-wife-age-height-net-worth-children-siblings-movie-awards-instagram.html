Shemar Moore Biography: Wife, Age, Height, Net Worth, Children, Siblings, Movie, Awards, Instagram

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, July 21, 2024

Shemar Moore is a talented actor who has graced television and cinema screens for over two decades.

He is also a former fashion model who has appeared in several music videos and magazines.

He is best known for his roles on the soap opera The Young and the Restless, the crime drama Criminal Minds, and the action series S.W.A.T.

Early Life & Education

Shemar Moore was born on April 20, 1970, in Oakland, California, to Marilyn Wilson, a teacher, and Sherrod Moore, a business consultant.

He has a younger half-brother named Stephen Wilson from his mother’s second marriage. He is of mixed ethnicity; his father is African-American, and his mother is of Irish and French-Canadian descent.

Moore spent his early years traveling with his mother, who worked overseas in Denmark, Bahrain, Ghana, and Greece.

He attended a British private school in Bahrain until he was seven. He then moved back to the U.S. and settled in Palo Alto, California, where he attended Gunn High School.

He was an athletic and popular student who played varsity baseball as a pitcher and an outfielder. He also developed a love for acting and participated in several school plays and theater productions.

He graduated from high school in 1988 and enrolled at Santa Clara University, where he majored in communications and minored in theater arts. He continued to play baseball in college and aspired to become a professional player.

However, he suffered a shoulder injury that ended his baseball career. He then focused on his studies and graduated in 1992 with a Bachelor of Arts.

Personal Life

Shemar Moore has been in several romantic relationships throughout his life. He dated actress Halle Berry from 1997 to 1998 and singer Toni Braxton from 1999 to 2000.

He was also linked to actresses Kimberly Elise, Ashley Scott, and Lauriane Gillieron. He was engaged to soccer player Shawna Gordon from 2014 to 2015, but they broke up due to irreconcilable differences.

He is currently in a relationship with Jesiree Dizon, a model he met in 2018. They have been dating for six years and are very happy together. They often share their love and affection on social media and attend various events as a couple.

He has one child, a son named Shemar Jr., who was born in 2020. He is very proud of his son and loves spending time with him.

He also considers his son his motivation and inspiration to work harder and achieve more in his career.

Career

Shemar Moore started his career as a model and appeared in several music videos for artists such as Mariah Carey, Toni Braxton, and Destiny’s Child. He also posed for magazines such as GQ, Essence, and Men’s Fitness.

He landed his breakthrough role as Malcolm Winters on The Young and the Restless in 1994. He played the charismatic and charming half-brother of Neil Winters, who had a tumultuous relationship with his family and love interests.

He won a Daytime Emmy Award and several N.A.A.C.P. Image Awards for his performance. He left the show in 2005 but returned for 2014, 2019, and 2023 guest appearances.

He also hosted the show Soul Train from 1999 to 2003, where he showcased his charisma and dancing skills. He interviewed various celebrities and musicians and introduced the latest trends and styles in music and fashion.

He starred as Derek Morgan on the crime drama Criminal Minds from 2005 to 2016. He played a skilled and loyal F.B.I. agent who specialized in explosives, fixations, and obsessive behaviors.

He was a fan favorite and formed a close bond with his co-stars, especially Kirsten Vangsness, who played his best friend and technical analyst, Penelope Garcia.

He left the show in 2016 to pursue other opportunities but reprised his role for the series finale in 2020. He has also appeared in movies such as The Brothers, Diary of a Mad Black Woman, and The Bounce Back. He made his debut as a producer and director with the latter, which was a romantic comedy that he also starred in.

He has starred as Daniel “Hondo” Harrelson on the action series S.W.A.T. since 2017. He plays the leader of a specialized tactical unit in Los Angeles, who balances his professional and personal life.

He is also a producer and occasional director for the show based on the 1975 series and the 2003 film of the same name.

Awards

Shemar Moore has won several awards and nominations for his work in television and film. Some of the notable ones are:

  • Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series for The Young and the Restless in 2000.
  • N.A.A.C.P. Image Award for Outstanding Actor in a Daytime Drama Series for The Young and the Restless in 1996, 1997, 1998, 2000, 2001, and 2002.
  • N.A.A.C.P. Image Award for Outstanding Actor in a Drama Series for Criminal Minds 2015.
  • People’s Choice Award for Favorite Crime Drama T.V. Actor for Criminal Minds in 2016 and 2017.
  • Teen Choice Award for Choice T.V. Actor: Action for S.W.A.T. in 2018 and 2019.

Controversy

Shemar Moore has faced controversy and criticism in his career and personal life. Some of the instances are:

  • In 1999, he was arrested for driving under the influence of alcohol and was sentenced to 36 months of probation and community service.
  • In 2004, he was accused of sexually assaulting a woman at his home, but the charges were dropped due to insufficient evidence.
  • In 2007, he was involved in a brawl with a fellow actor at the Daytime Emmy Awards, which resulted in minor injuries and a lawsuit.
  • In 2014, he was rumored to be dating his co-star Kirsten Vangsness, but they denied the allegations and clarified that they were just friends.
  • In 2017, he was criticized for making insensitive comments about his biracial identity and his preference for light-skinned women, which he later apologized for and explained that he was misunderstood.
