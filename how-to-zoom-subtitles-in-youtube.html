How To Zoom Subtitles In Youtube

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, July 6, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

Struggling to Decipher YouTube Subtitles? Fear Not, Fellow Subtitle Squinter!

Ah, subtitles. Those magical lines of text that dance across the screen, translating the wisdom (or nonsense) of foreign films or deciphering the mumbles of actors determined to sound deep despite their muffled delivery. But sometimes, those subtitles can be the size of ants at a picnic – you practically need an electron microscope to read them. Fear not, fellow subtitle squinter, for I am here to guide you on this quest for gigantic text!

Enlarging the Microscopic: Your Guide to YouTube Subtitle Zoomery

First things first, are you on your computer or your phone? This subtitle zoom adventure will take a slightly different path depending on your device.

Onward, Desktop Warriors!

For our desktop comrades, the path to giant subtitles is paved with clicks. While the video is playing, find the little gear icon in the bottom right corner (next to the volume control) and click it. A menu will appear, and nestled amongst its options will be that glorious savior: Subtitles/CC. But wait, there's more! Click the word "Options" next to Subtitles/CC, and a treasure trove of text customization awaits! Here you can freely adjust the font size using a handy slider – bigger is always better in this case, folks! You can also play around with the font style, color, and opacity to create the perfect subtitle experience (because who doesn't love a little rainbow text action?).

Conquering the Mobile Subtitle Mountain

For our mobile YouTube masters, the quest is a little different. Tap your profile picture in the top right corner, and then delve into the mysterious world of Settings. Once there, find the Captions section – it's like a secret handshake for subtitle enthusiasts! Within this sacred space, you'll find the key to giant subtitles: Caption size and style. Here you can adjust the size to your heart's content, ensuring those subtitles are billboard-worthy on your tiny screen.

But Officer, What About Keyboard Shortcuts?

Why yes, there are those too! For the truly subtitle-savvy individual, a keyboard shortcut can be your best friend. While the video is playing on your computer, press the C key to turn captions on or off. To increase the size, use the plus (+) key, and to decrease it, use the minus (-) key. Simple, right?

So You've Conquered Subtitle Smallness, But Do Questions Linger?

Fear not, for I have compiled a list of Frequently Asked Subtitle-Enlargement Questions (because yes, they exist!):

How to turn subtitles on altogether?

While the video is playing, click the CC button in the bottom right corner (desktop) or tap the CC icon at the bottom of the video (mobile).

Can I change the subtitle language?

Absolutely! Click the CC button and select your desired language from the list.

Help! My subtitles are HUGE!

Don't worry, we've all been there. Just follow the same steps above and adjust the font size back down to a reasonable level.

Why can't I find the option to change the subtitle size?

This option might not be available for all videos, especially if the creator hasn't uploaded their own captions.

Is there a way to make the subtitles appear faster or slower?

Unfortunately, there isn't a built-in option to adjust the speed of the subtitles themselves. However, you can adjust the playback speed of the entire video.

Now go forth and conquer those tiny subtitles! With this newfound knowledge, you can finally enjoy YouTube videos without needing an eagle eye (or a magnifying glass).
