How To Get A Friends List On Animal Crossing

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, July 31, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

So You Want to Be BFFs in Animal Crossing: A Totally Tubular Guide to Friend-Making

Ah, Animal Crossing: a delightful world of adorable animals, chill vibes, and...crippling loneliness if you don't have any friends to share it with. But fear not, fellow islander! This guide will turn you from a social butterfly in a net to a social butterfly with a whole dang butterfly net collection, all thanks to the power of friendship (and maybe some Nook Miles).

Step 1: Accepting You're Not Tom Hanks in Cast Away (But That's Okay!)

Look, we've all been there. You boot up the game, spruce up your island paradise, and then...crickets. No Tammy telling you about her knee replacement surgery, no Blathers rambling about creepy crawlies (though that might be a plus). The island life can feel a bit deserted without some pals. But don't worry, befriending folks in Animal Crossing is easier than catching a coelacanth with a fishing rod (although that's pretty darn easy too).

Step 2: Acquisition of the All-Important Best Friends List App

This fancy app on your Nook Phone is basically your social media platform for the cutest game ever. But unlike Facebook, you won't get bombarded with farm requests or weird political rants from your uncle. No, here it's all sunshine and virtual beaches. Unlocking this app requires a little legwork, though. You gotta chat up Orville at Dodo Airlines and convince him you're worthy of having online friends. Don't worry, it's not like some high school popularity contest. Just be yourself (and maybe bring some snacks; Orville seems like a snack kinda guy).

Step 3: How to Actually Make Friends (without Being a Creep)

Alright, listen up, because this is where things get real. Here are some tips to transform you from a social awkwardness simulator to a friend-making machine:

  • Embrace the Dodo Airlines Open Door Policy: Head back to Orville and tell him to open the gates for "All My Friends" (air quotes optional). This lets anyone on your Switch friend list visit your island. Just be warned: it's like inviting everyone you've ever met to your house party. There's a chance your island will be overrun by weirdos who steal your turnips. But hey, at least it'll be an interesting story for your future memoirs (or, you know, your next Animal Crossing therapy session).
  • The Refined Approach: Selective Best Friending: This is where the Best Friends List App comes in handy. Once you play with someone online (by visiting their island or having them visit yours), they'll appear on this list. Here's the key: You can choose to formally request them as a Best Friend. This is basically the in-game equivalent of a friendship bracelet exchange, but way cooler (and less itchy). Just be sure they're someone you vibe with; you don't want some random person running around picking all your flowers.

Remember: Friendship is a two-way street! Be a good host, send some gifts (everyone loves a pineapple surprise!), and don't be afraid to chat with your newfound comrades.

Step 4: The Joys (and Potential Perils) of Friendship

Now that you've got a squad, it's time to reap the rewards! You can:

  • Trade items and resources: Need that elusive golden watering can? Your best bud might have one lying around (or at least a spare DIY recipe).
  • Explore each other's islands: Every island is unique, and who knows? You might discover a hidden gem (or a terrifying collection of creepy crawlies in your friend's basement).
  • Fish, catch bugs, and raid those money rocks together: Basically, all the fun stuff is even more fun with friends!

But beware, friend-osaur! Having friends also comes with some risks:

  • Friend-turned-Foe: Just like in real life, online friendships can sour. Maybe your friend keeps accidentally (or maybe not-so-accidentally) chopping down your trees with their fancy golden axe. If things go south, you can always revoke their Best Friend status. It's basically the friend-zone on steroids.
  • The Pressure to be Perfect: Your friends might have these amazingly decorated islands, while yours looks like a toddler decorated it with glitter and glue. Don't compare yourself! Everyone progresses at their own pace. Besides, a little chaos is what makes Animal Crossing interesting, right?

There you have it! With a little effort and these handy tips, you'll be building sandcastles of friendship with the best of them. Now get out there
