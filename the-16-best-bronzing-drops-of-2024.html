The 16 Best Bronzing Drops of 2024

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, September 29, 2024

There’s just something about sun-kissed skin that implies vitality and health — but we all know that spending time outside without the protection of SPF isn’t good for us. But when you’re feeling lackluster, sometimes a dusting of powder bronzer isn’t enough to get the job done, and often doesn’t create the hyper-realistic look of truly glowing skin.

The solution here? Bronzing drops. These clever formulas are little boosters that you can add to your favorite skincare formulas or even apply directly to skin to add radiance and glow, whether you’re looking for something that’ll wash off at the end of the night or last for several days.

There are many options on the market, ranging from makeup to self-tanner, and some even target specific skin concerns, like bronzing drops that are compatible with oily skin or won’t call attention to fine lines and wrinkles. Yet the true magic of bronzing drops are how customizable they are — you can create your own perfect shade by the number of drops you use — and how ridiculously easy they are to use.

What We Don’t Love: You’ll need to apply more than 12 drops to get SPF protection, which may be too sparkly for some.

TAN-LUXE is known for its collection of high-quality, hyper-realistic glow formulas; while this product doesn’t feature a self tanner, it does provide an instant sun-kissed glow as well as luminosity with just a few drops. If that’s your jam, you’ll love it even more when you realize that the treatment is formulated with SPF to shield your skin from future sun damage.

While the formula may look very intense coming out of the dropper, the metallic effect quickly diffuses into a sheer radiant bronze that never looks orange, even on fair skin, with the help of a brush. The hydrating and nourishing base sinks into skin to create a silky glide to the skin without feeling greasy, thanks to the addition of squalane, an emollient that acts like your natural sebum.

It’s worth noting that while many people use glow drops to add a subtle shimmer to their skincare regimen, you’ll need to use at least 12 drops to get adequate sun protection. This may result in a look that’s too high-beam for some, but for those who appreciate a bit of drama (or don’t mind using fewer drops with a separate SPF), this product from TAN-LUXE is a phenomenal pick.

What to Keep in Mind

Your Skin Type

As is the case with any beauty product, you’ll want to make sure the bronzing drops you buy are compatible with your skin tone as well as type. Regarding tone, think about the desired outcome. “I always advise clients to use something that will give them a warm glow and build gradually over time just as a natural tan would,” says Thomas.

When it comes to your skin type, consider the texture and finish of the drops before buying them. If you have dry skin, you’ll want a more moisturizing or oil-based formula, like Caliray Just Add Rays Universal Translucent Glow Booster Drops and RéVive Glow Elixir Hydrating Radiance Oil — these will add nourishment to your skin. Ingredients like hyaluronic acid (found in Pai The Impossible Glow Drops) and glycerin will also help draw moisture into your skin.

Oily skin types will want to look for oil-free formulas that feature ingredients that will help regulate oil production, like niacinamide (a key ingredient in Danessa Myricks Beauty Yummy Skin Glow Serum with Hyaluronic Acid). You may also want to look for a matte finish, like that of PerriconeMD No Makeup Bronzer SPF 15. “If you are oily, be careful when using luminous formulas that add sheen to the skin,” says Hart. “Products that contain mica as it may enhance the shine on the skin.”

Customizable Tan

All bronzing drops are completely customizable, but when it comes to tanning drops, you’ll want to ease your way in, starting with a lower number of drops and then building up once you get a sense of how the color looks on your skin. “As a general guideline, we recommend that you start with a small number of drops — two to three — for a subtle, natural-looking tan,” says Claghorn. “If you prefer a deeper tan, you can gradually increase the number of drops used.”

Hart adds that “building your tan gradually will ensure you don’t end up feeling too dark or with an uneven base.”

The ease of using tanning drops is that you just mix them with your favorite skincare products. Claghorn recommends picking a serum, moisturizer, or oil that is lightweight, spreads easily, and is compatible with your skin type to achieve the best results. You should do this in the palm of your hand, rather than on your body or in the bottle, so you can best control the tan.

“Avoid mixing tanning drops with products that contain active ingredients like retinol or exfoliating acids, as they may interfere with the tanning process or cause uneven results,” she warns.

To build a gradual tan, start with just a few drops to achieve a subtle hint of color and assess it 24 hours after your application. Then, increase the number of drops gradually. “In subsequent applications, you can add an additional one to two extra drops to your mixture,” says Claghorn. “Keep an eye on how your skin responds to each application and adjust the number of drops accordingly — this approach allows you to achieve a natural-looking, gradual tan without any sudden or dramatic changes.”

Once you’ve hit your desired color, maintain it by applying your custom blend every couple of days. Don’t forget to exfoliate a few times a week — this will make sure your tan wears evenly and won’t look patchy or spotty.

Ingredients

You’ll see a lot of overlap in bronzing drop formulas, like mica to add luminosity in cosmetic products and/or DHA in self-tanners to create that faux glow effect. You’ll also find plenty of unique products with skincare actives and one of our favorites to look for is vitamin C. In addition to being a potent antioxidant, it helps brighten up a dull complexion and can fade hyperpigmentation — so essentially, you’ll be combatting sun damage while faking a sun-kissed look.

Whether or not you look for a “clean” formula is up to you. But remember — your skin is your biggest organ. “Everything you put on it absorbs and affects you,” cautions Thomas. “The FDA really only regulates what is put into nail products and hair dyes, but not self-tanners or products that alter skin tone, so you need to read the ingredient label and make sure that what you are applying is not going to affect you negatively.”

In particular, Claghorn recommends keeping an eye out for cosmetic preservatives, specifically parabens. “The main reason some individuals choose to avoid methylparaben and propylparaben is due to their potential endocrine-disrupting properties,” she says. Endocrine disruptors can negatively interact with your body’s hormonal function. While more studies are needed, it’s often better to be safe rather than sorry.

Your Questions, Answered

What do bronzing drops do?

It’s right in the name — they add a drop of bronze. “They are a concentrated liquid bronzer that can easily mix into your skincare or makeup to customize your level of tan,” says Hart. “They’re an easy and quick way to achieve a sun-kissed look without damaging your skin in the sun.”

The beauty of bronzing drops is that you can create a wide range of looks by using them in a variety of ways. Thomas likes applying them to where you’d place a bronzer — the high points of the face — to mimic the effect of having been on a sunny vacation.

There are also a variety of bronzing drop options to choose from. “Some are luminizing and give the skin a beautiful glow, while other bronzing drops dry more matte and are perfect for oily skin types,” says Hart. There are also some bronzing drops that are self-tanner.

Are bronzing drops fake tan?

Sometimes; you can find bronzing drops in makeup, self-tanning, and even hybrid formulas.

“Tanning glow drops contain self-tanning ingredients that interact with the top layer of the skin to create a temporary tan appearance,” explains Claghorn. “They offer a safer alternative to traditional tanning methods and allow individuals to achieve a desired tan without sun exposure.”

When shopping for bronzing drops, it’s important to read the packaging to make sure you’re getting exactly what you want. “Usually, if the package only says ‘bronzer,’ it will not contain self-tanning ingredients that will actually darken the skin,” says Hart.

How do you darken your foundation with bronzer drops?

Mixing bronzing drops into your foundation will add a nice shimmer, similar to a cream bronzer, giving your foundation a glowy finish and deepening the shade so you can stick with using only one foundation color all season long.

“It’s a great product to throw in your bag when you are going on vacation so that you can adapt your foundation as you have more time in the sun,” says Thomas.

Hart recommends starting with one drop and continuing to add until you have the depth you are looking for — try using the back of your hand as a mixing palette.

Before you start playing cosmetic chemist, though, take a look at the ingredient lists of your foundation and bronzing drops. “Most bronzing drops typically have oil in them, so when they’re mixed with a water-based foundation, it is important to note that they will not play well together,” says Thomas. “Any foundation that is oil based will work seamlessly with it and actually sheer out the foundation a bit to give it a more natural skin finish.”

Bondi Sands Pure Self-Tanning Drops, Tanologist Face and Body Drops, and Drunk Elephant D-Bronzi Anti-Pollution Sunshine Drops are all water-based, so they should play well with your water-based foundations.

How do you apply tanning drops?

The key to success with tanning drops is to start with the proper base — clean, exfoliated, and dry skin. If you apply lotions before mixing up your tanning drop concoction, the tan will be unable to stick to the skin and could create a patchy look.

Blending your drops of choice into moisturizer, serum, cream, or oil and apply evenly in circular motions is the tried-and-true application method. “Work them well into the neck, around your ears and into your hairline to ensure you get an even tan,” says Hart.

After you’ve slathered yourself in bronze, wash your hands so they don’t stain! “If you’re rinsing the tanned area within two to eight hours of application, do not use soap and do not apply cosmetic products until the tan is fully developed,” says Claghorn. Otherwise, you risk washing away your tan. After that, you can use soap.
