Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, July 7, 2024
The biggest wingspan of any bird can be found on the Wondering Albatross

Wondering Albatross

The wandering albatross has the longest wingspan of any living bird, typically ranging from 2.51 to 3.5 m (8 ft 3 in to 11 ft 6 in), with a mean span of 3.1 m (10 ft 2 in) in the Bird Island, South Georgia colony and an average of exactly 3 m (9 ft 10 in) in 123 birds measured off the coast of Malabar, New South Wales.

Is there a bird that never lands?

The scientific name for the common swift, A. apus, means “without feet” and refers to their extremely short legs. The common swift uses its legs only to cling to vertical surfaces, as swifts typically never land on the ground as they would be too exposed to predators.
