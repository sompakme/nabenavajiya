Early Life and Education

Herb Kohl was born on February 7, 1935, in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He grew up in a Jewish family and was raised with a strong sense of community and philanthropy. Kohl attended Washington High School in Milwaukee and later graduated from the University of Wisconsin, where he earned a Bachelor of Science degree. He furthered his education by obtaining a Master of Business Administration from Harvard Business School.

Business Ventures

After completing his education, Kohl took over his family-owned business, Kohl’s grocery stores, which eventually expanded into a nationwide chain of department stores. His business acumen led to the growth of the company, and in 1979, the Kohl family sold the corporation to BATUS Inc., which provided a significant boost to Herb Kohl’s personal wealth.

Political Career

Kohl’s interest in public service led him to a successful political career. He served as a United States Senator from Wisconsin from 1989 to 2013. During his tenure, he was known for his work on the Judiciary Committee, the Appropriations Committee, and his advocacy for consumer protection and antitrust laws. His political career did not significantly increase his net worth but did raise his profile as a public figure.

Ownership of the Milwaukee Bucks

In 1985, Herb Kohl purchased the NBA’s Milwaukee Bucks to ensure the team remained in his home state of Wisconsin. He owned the team for nearly three decades before selling it in 2014 for a reported $550 million. This sale was a major contributor to his net worth.

Philanthropic Efforts
