Nigerian Woman Pours Out Her Mind After Chimamanda Receives Praise from Core Critics Over Election L

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, August 2, 2024
  • A Nigerian woman has shared her thoughts about the reception Chimamanda Adichie received after she wrote a letter to the US President
  • The lady said the globally recognised novelist was widely criticised in Nigeria for her feminist stance
  • She added that reason the feminist always received backlash from Nigerians was that she does not appeal to their sentiments

A Nigerian woman who identified as Ijeoma on Facebook has noted that some people who agreed with Chimamanda Adichie after she wrote a letter to President Biden about the Nigerian election were formerly her core critics.

The lady explained that many who disliked Adichie's feminist stance only do so because it does not appeal to their existing sentiments.

Adichie is a writer, public speaker and feminist who has spoken out about the importance of feminism in society.

Adichie has also spoken about the importance of intersectionality in feminism and has argued that feminism should be inclusive of women of all races and backgrounds.

She is now a hero

Ijeoma revealed that celebrating the globally recognised novelist now was a direct portrayal of hypocrisy.

Her words:

"Some Nigerian men that dragged Adichie and called her so many horrifying names cos she is a feminist suddenly turning around and praising her cos she wrote an open letter about Obi. They are suddenly posting her and praising her. Calling her odeluwa, queen, amazing, truth speaker, etc. Person wey una call deceiver, bitter and called her husband a simp. In this Nigeria, just have data. You will see it all."
