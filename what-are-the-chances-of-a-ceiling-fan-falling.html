Therefore, the issue is whether or not it is unsafe for a ceiling fan to wobble.

When operating on high, it is usual to have a minor wobbling of around 1/8″ in diameter. Anything more than that may cause damage to the fan, and depending on the source of the wobbling, it can even be potentially harmful to the user.

In a similar vein, how can I tell if my ceiling fan is about to come crashing down?

Blades that are shaky It is possible for your ceiling fan’s blades to begin to wobble while it rotates if the fan loses its equilibrium during operation. Eventually, a wobbling ceiling fan may be in danger of falling from the ceiling and causing injury or death to you and your loved ones.

What is causing my ceiling fan to wobble?

Ceiling fan wobbling may be caused by imbalances in the fan blades or blade holders, misalignment of the blades, excessive dust collection, or simply by loose blade holder mounting screws on the fan blades or blade holders. Repair your fan to its smooth-running state before you wear down the working components of the fan.
