Bringing Your Pew-Pew to the Big Apple: A Not-So-Bright Idea

Thinking of packing heat for your trip to New York City? Well, hold your horses (or should we say, holsters?) because bringing a firearm to the city that never sleeps is about as easy as snagging a taxi in rush hour.

The Big No-No of Bringing a Boom Stick

New York City has some of the strictest gun laws in the country. Like, stricter than a bouncer at a velvet rope club. Owning a gun requires a special license, and forget about carrying it around with you â€“ that's a big N-O-P-E. Unless you're packing serious heat for a Broadway play (looking at you, Chicago!), leave the firepower at home.

So, What are You Supposed to Do if a Pigeon Attacks?

Okay, okay, pigeon attacks are a rare occurrence (though they do have excellent dive-bombing skills). New Yorkers are a pretty tough bunch, and they've got their ways of dealing with street crime. Here's your survival guide (minus the weapons):

  • The Side-Step Shuffle: This involves a masterful display of footwork that allows you to gracefully avoid any suspicious activity. Think Michael Jackson's moonwalk, but with less sequins.
  • The NYC Stare: This is a powerful tool. A withering glance can make even the most brazen petty thief reconsider their life choices.
  • The Buddy System: There's safety in numbers, people! Stick with a friend or group, especially at night.

But I Have a Permit! Can't I Bring My Little Buddy?

Nope. New York City has its own set of rules, and even out-of-state permits need a special NYC stamp of approval, which is notoriously hard to get. Unless you're best friends with the mayor (and even then, it's a maybe), leave the firearm at home.

FAQ: Bringing a Gun to NYC (Spoiler Alert: It's a No-Go)

How to apply for a gun license in NYC?

Don't bother. The process is long, complicated, and requires a reason that most visitors wouldn't qualify for.

How can I protect myself in NYC?

Stay alert, use common sense, and trust the Side-Step Shuffle (see above).

What if I see a crime happening?

Call 911! The NYPD may not be packing heat for every encounter, but they have the training and the firepower to handle things.

Can I bring a BB gun/paintball gun/toy gun?

Nope. NYC takes a dim view of anything that even resembles a real firearm. Play it safe and leave the pretend guns at home too.

Is pepper spray legal?

Nope again. Self-defense laws in NYC are pretty restrictive. Stick to the Side-Step Shuffle and the NYC Stare.

Remember, New York City is an amazing place with plenty to see and do. Leave the guns at home, and focus on enjoying the sights, sounds, and maybe even a slice of pizza (because that's a weapon of deliciousness everyone can enjoy).
